GEORGIA MEDICAID / DEPARTMENT OF COMMUNITY HEALTH
Provider Manual: Claims Submission and Processing Guidelines
Effective: State Fiscal Year 2025

SUBJECT: Medicaid Claims Filing Requirements and Common Denial Resolutions

1. ELIGIBILITY VERIFICATION

1.1 Medicaid eligibility must be verified for EVERY encounter:
   - Use the Georgia MMIS Web Portal (www.mmis.georgia.gov)
   - Check eligibility on the date of service, not just the date of scheduling
   - Verify: active status, managed care plan assignment, benefit limits, TPL (third party liability)

1.2 Retroactive Eligibility: Georgia Medicaid may grant retroactive eligibility up to 3 months prior to the application date. Providers may submit claims for dates of service during the retroactive period within 365 days of the eligibility determination date.

2. CLAIMS SUBMISSION

2.1 Filing Deadlines:
   - Fee-for-service Medicaid: 365 days from date of service
   - Managed Care (CMOs): Varies by plan (typically 90-180 days)
     * Amerigroup: 180 days
     * Peach State Health Plan: 180 days
     * CareSource: 180 days
     * WellCare: 120 days

2.2 Required Claim Elements:
   - Patient Medicaid ID number (13 digits)
   - Provider Medicaid ID and NPI
   - Referring provider NPI (for specialist and diagnostic services)
   - ICD-10 diagnosis codes to highest specificity
   - CPT/HCPCS procedure codes with required modifiers
   - Place of service code
   - Prior authorization number (when applicable)

2.3 Electronic Submission:
   - Payer ID: 61148090000 (Georgia Medicaid)
   - Required for all providers billing >$5,000 annually
   - HIPAA-compliant 837P/837I format

3. COMMON DENIAL CODES AND RESOLUTIONS

3.1 CO-22 (Care may be covered by another payer):
   - Indicates third party liability (TPL) exists
   - Georgia Medicaid is ALWAYS the payer of last resort
   - Resolution: Bill the primary payer first, then submit to Medicaid with the primary payer's EOB
   - If patient has no other coverage, submit TPL override form

3.2 CO-197 (Prior authorization required):
   - Georgia Medicaid requires prior auth for:
     * All inpatient admissions (non-emergency)
     * Outpatient surgeries >$2,500
     * Advanced imaging (MRI, CT, PET)
     * DME exceeding $500
     * Behavioral health services beyond initial assessment
   - Retrospective authorization available for emergencies (request within 2 business days)
   - Authorization portal: www.mmis.georgia.gov/priorauth

3.3 CO-29 (Timely filing):
   - Claims denied for timely filing cannot be billed to the Medicaid member
   - Appeals must include proof of timely submission (clearinghouse report, electronic confirmation)
   - If the delay was caused by another payer (COB), include the other payer's EOB showing processing date

3.4 CO-16 (Missing information):
   - Common for claims missing: Medicaid ID, referring provider, diagnosis codes
   - Corrected claims must be resubmitted within 60 days of the denial
   - Use claim frequency code 7 (replacement) for corrected claims

4. MANAGED CARE ORGANIZATION (MCO) DENIALS

4.1 Georgia Medicaid members are predominantly enrolled in Care Management Organizations (CMOs). When a member is assigned to a CMO, claims must be submitted to the CMO, not fee-for-service Medicaid.

4.2 CMO Denial Appeals:
   - First level: Internal appeal to the CMO (30 days to file)
   - Second level: State Fair Hearing through DCH (120 days to request)
   - Members have the right to request continuation of services during appeal

4.3 CMO Authorization Denials:
   - CMOs must respond to standard authorization requests within 14 days
   - Expedited requests must be responded to within 72 hours
   - If CMO fails to respond timely, the service is deemed authorized

5. GRADY-SPECIFIC MEDICAID CONSIDERATIONS

5.1 Disproportionate Share Hospital (DSH): Grady qualifies for DSH supplemental payments. Accurate coding and complete claims are essential to maximize DSH eligibility calculations.

5.2 340B Drug Pricing: Claims for drugs acquired under the 340B program must use modifier UD on outpatient pharmacy claims and modifier JG on physician-administered drugs.

5.3 Presumptive Eligibility: Grady is a qualified entity for Medicaid Presumptive Eligibility. Patients screened as likely eligible may receive coverage for up to 60 days while the full application is processed.
