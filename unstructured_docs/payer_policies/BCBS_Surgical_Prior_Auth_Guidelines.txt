SUBJECT: Prior Authorization Requirements for Elective Surgical Procedures

1. SCOPE

This policy applies to all elective surgical procedures performed in outpatient, ambulatory surgery center, and inpatient settings when the expected total charges exceed $5,000. Emergency and urgent procedures are exempt from prior authorization but must be reported within 48 hours.

2. PRIOR AUTHORIZATION REQUIREMENTS

2.1 Submission Timeline: Authorization requests must be submitted at least 14 business days before the scheduled procedure date for elective cases. Expedited reviews are available for urgent (non-emergent) cases within 72 hours.

2.2 Required Documentation:
   a) Procedure CPT code(s) and detailed description
   b) ICD-10 diagnosis code(s) with clinical rationale
   c) Clinical rationale and supporting medical records including:
      - History and physical examination dated within 30 days
      - Relevant diagnostic test results (imaging, labs, pathology)
      - Conservative treatment history documenting minimum duration:
        * Orthopedic procedures: 6 weeks of conservative therapy
        * Spine procedures: 12 weeks of conservative therapy
        * Bariatric procedures: 6 months supervised weight management
   d) Imaging results dated within 90 days of the requested procedure
   e) Specialist consultation notes
   f) For total joint replacement: documentation of failed conservative measures including physical therapy, NSAIDs, and corticosteroid injections

2.3 Specific Procedure Requirements:

   Total Knee Replacement (CPT 27447):
   - BMI documentation (BMI > 40 may require additional clearance)
   - X-ray evidence of Kellgren-Lawrence Grade 3 or 4 osteoarthritis
   - Minimum 6 weeks physical therapy documentation
   - Trial of at least 2 different NSAID medications
   - At least 1 corticosteroid injection with documented response

   Total Hip Replacement (CPT 27130):
   - X-ray evidence of joint space narrowing or avascular necrosis
   - Functional assessment score (Harris Hip Score or equivalent)
   - Documentation of impact on activities of daily living

   Laparoscopic Cholecystectomy (CPT 47562):
   - Ultrasound confirming cholelithiasis or cholecystitis
   - Documentation of symptoms (biliary colic, right upper quadrant pain)
   - Labs including liver function tests and CBC

3. DENIAL CODES AND RESOLUTION

3.1 CO-197 (Prior authorization/notification required): This is the most common surgical denial. Prevention strategies:
   - Always verify authorization requirements using the BCBS provider portal
   - Obtain authorization BEFORE scheduling the procedure
   - Document the authorization number on the claim form (Box 23 on CMS-1500)
   - Verify that the authorized CPT code matches the performed procedure exactly
   - If the procedure scope changes intraoperatively, notify BCBS within 24 hours

3.2 CO-234 (Not medically necessary): Common when conservative treatment documentation is insufficient. Ensure medical records clearly document:
   - Duration and type of conservative treatment
   - Patient's functional limitations
   - Progression of condition despite treatment
   - Specific clinical criteria met for the requested procedure

4. APPEAL PROCESS

First Level Appeal: Submit within 180 days of denial.
   Required: Written appeal letter, clinical documentation, peer-reviewed evidence
   Timeline: Decision within 30 calendar days

Second Level Appeal: Submit within 60 days of first level denial.
   Required: Additional clinical evidence, specialist letter of medical necessity
   Timeline: Decision within 45 calendar days

External Review: Available for medical necessity denials after exhausting internal appeals.
   Filed through the Georgia Department of Insurance
   Timeline: Decision within 45 calendar days
