CENTERS FOR MEDICARE & MEDICAID SERVICES (CMS)
Medicare Claims Processing Manual - Timely Filing Requirements
Publication: CMS Pub 100-04, Chapter 1, Section 70
Effective: January 1, 2024
Last Updated: October 2024

SUBJECT: Timely Filing Requirements and Denial Code CO-29

1. STATUTORY FILING DEADLINES

1.1 Original Medicare (Parts A and B):
   - Claims must be filed within 12 calendar months (365 days) of the date of service
   - For services spanning multiple dates, the filing deadline begins from the last date of service
   - Calendar year rule: Claims for services provided in 2024 must be filed by December 31, 2025

1.2 Medicare Advantage (Part C) Plans:
   - Filing deadlines vary by plan, typically 90-180 days from date of service
   - Providers must verify the specific deadline with each Medicare Advantage plan
   - Common Medicare Advantage timely filing deadlines:
     * Humana Medicare: 365 days
     * UnitedHealthcare Medicare: 365 days
     * Aetna Medicare: 365 days
     * Blue Cross Medicare: 90 days (varies by state)

1.3 Medicare Part D (Prescription Drugs):
   - Pharmacy claims: Must be submitted at point of sale
   - Paper claims: Within 36 months of the date of service

2. DENIAL CODE CO-29 (TIMELY FILING LIMIT EXCEEDED)

2.1 This denial code indicates the claim was received after the applicable filing deadline. Important: CO-29 denials CANNOT be appealed on the basis of medical necessity.

2.2 Valid Appeal Bases for CO-29:
   - Proof of timely original submission (clearinghouse transmission confirmation)
   - Documentation that the delay was caused by the payer (e.g., incorrect member ID provided)
   - Retroactive Medicare eligibility determination
   - Natural disaster or other extraordinary circumstance

2.3 Invalid Appeal Bases (will be rejected):
   - Provider was unaware of filing deadline
   - Internal billing system issues
   - Staff turnover or vacation
   - Medical necessity of the service

3. PREVENTION STRATEGIES

3.1 Submission Best Practices:
   a) Submit all claims within 48 hours of the date of service
   b) Monitor claim status at 14-day intervals using:
      - Medicare Administrative Contractor (MAC) provider portal
      - Interactive Voice Response (IVR) system
      - HIPAA 276/277 electronic claim status inquiry
   c) Set automated alerts for claims approaching filing deadlines:
      - 60-day warning for claims not yet acknowledged
      - 30-day warning for claims denied or rejected and not yet corrected
      - 14-day warning for claims approaching the absolute filing deadline

3.2 Documentation Retention:
   - Maintain clearinghouse transmission logs as proof of submission date
   - Keep electronic acknowledgment receipts (999, TA1)
   - Archive claim status inquiry results
   - Document all phone contacts with payer (date, time, representative name, reference number)

3.3 Common Scenarios Leading to CO-29:
   a) Claims submitted to wrong payer due to incorrect eligibility verification
   b) Claims rejected for errors and not corrected/resubmitted timely
   c) Claims held pending medical records that were never obtained
   d) Coordination of benefits (COB) delays when primary payer processing is slow
   e) Retroactive termination of patient's coverage

4. CORRECTED CLAIMS AND ADJUSTMENTS

4.1 Corrected claims are NOT subject to the original filing deadline IF:
   - The original claim was timely filed
   - The corrected claim is submitted within 12 months of the original claim's processing date
   - The corrected claim references the original claim number

4.2 Late charges (additional line items) must be submitted as a new claim and ARE subject to the original filing deadline.

5. STATE PROMPT PAYMENT LAWS

Georgia Prompt Payment Act (O.C.G.A. § 33-24-59.5):
   - Clean claims must be paid within 15 business days (electronic) or 30 business days (paper)
   - Interest accrues at 18% per annum on late payments
   - Does not override timely filing requirements
