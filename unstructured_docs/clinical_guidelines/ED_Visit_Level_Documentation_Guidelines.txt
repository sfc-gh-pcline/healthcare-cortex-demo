SUBJECT: ED Visit Level Selection and Documentation Requirements for Billing Compliance

1. PURPOSE

This guideline establishes documentation requirements for Emergency Department evaluation and management (E/M) coding levels to ensure accurate billing, reduce claim denials, and maintain compliance with CMS and commercial payer requirements.

2. ED VISIT LEVELS AND DOCUMENTATION REQUIREMENTS

2.1 CPT 99281 - ED Visit Level 1 (Minor/Self-Limited):
   - Chief complaint documented
   - Brief history of present illness
   - Limited examination (1 body area or organ system)
   - Straightforward medical decision making
   - Examples: Simple laceration, insect bite, prescription refill
   - Expected denial rate: <1%

2.2 CPT 99282 - ED Visit Level 2 (Low Severity):
   - Chief complaint with HPI (1-3 elements)
   - Limited examination (1-5 body areas)
   - Low complexity medical decision making
   - Examples: Simple fracture, UTI, otitis media
   - Expected denial rate: 2%

2.3 CPT 99283 - ED Visit Level 3 (Moderate Severity):
   - Extended HPI (4+ elements or 3 chronic conditions)
   - Extended examination (6+ body areas)
   - Moderate complexity medical decision making
   - At least 2 of: moderate data reviewed, moderate risk, moderate diagnoses
   - Examples: Asthma exacerbation, cellulitis requiring IV antibiotics, abdominal pain with workup
   - Expected denial rate: 5%

2.4 CPT 99284 - ED Visit Level 4 (High Severity, No Immediate Threat):
   - Comprehensive HPI
   - Comprehensive examination (8+ body areas)
   - Moderate to high complexity medical decision making
   - Multiple diagnostic studies ordered and reviewed
   - Examples: Chest pain with cardiac workup, acute abdomen, TIA evaluation
   - Expected denial rate: 8%
   - Common denial reason: Insufficient documentation of medical decision making complexity

2.5 CPT 99285 - ED Visit Level 5 (High Severity, Immediate Threat):
   - Comprehensive HPI and examination
   - High complexity medical decision making
   - Immediate life-threatening condition requiring urgent intervention
   - Examples: STEMI, stroke, sepsis, trauma activation, respiratory failure
   - Expected denial rate: 12%
   - Common denial reasons: Diagnosis does not support level 5, insufficient documentation of threat to life

3. DOCUMENTATION BEST PRACTICES

3.1 Medical Decision Making (MDM) Documentation:
   MDM is the PRIMARY driver of ED visit level selection. Document:
   - Number and complexity of problems addressed
   - Amount and complexity of data reviewed (labs, imaging, EKGs, records)
   - Risk of complications, morbidity, or mortality
   - Clinical reasoning for each test ordered and treatment decision

3.2 Time-Based Documentation:
   - Time may be used as the controlling factor when counseling/coordination exceeds 50%
   - Document total time spent AND activities performed
   - Include time for: record review, care coordination, patient/family counseling

3.3 Critical Care Documentation (CPT 99291/99292):
   - Document total critical care time separately
   - Must specify the critical condition and what makes it life-threatening
   - Document moment-to-moment clinical decision making
   - Specify interventions: intubation, central line, vasopressors, etc.
   - Minimum 30 minutes of critical care for initial code (99291)

4. COMMON DENIAL SCENARIOS AND PREVENTION

4.1 Level 4/5 Downcoded to Level 3:
   - Cause: MDM documentation does not support the billed level
   - Prevention: Ensure the note documents at least 2 of 3 MDM elements at the billed level
   - Use the MDM grid as a reference when completing documentation

4.2 CO-11 (Diagnosis inconsistent with procedure):
   - Cause: ICD-10 code does not clinically support the ED level
   - Prevention: Use the most specific ICD-10 code; document acuity in the diagnosis
   - Example: Use R07.9 (chest pain, unspecified) for level 3, but I21.9 (AMI) for level 5

4.3 Observation vs. Inpatient Status Denials:
   - Medicare Two-Midnight Rule: Expected stay < 2 midnights = observation
   - Document expected length of stay and clinical reasoning for admission status
   - Physician attestation required within 24 hours of admission

5. QUALITY METRICS

The ED Coding Compliance Program monitors:
   - Distribution of visit levels (expected: 5% L1, 20% L2, 35% L3, 25% L4, 15% L5)
   - Denial rate by visit level
   - Documentation completion rate within 24 hours
   - Critical care documentation compliance
   - Provider-specific coding patterns

6. COMPLIANCE

Failure to document at the level billed constitutes a compliance risk. Providers who consistently code above documentation support will be:
   1. Notified by the Compliance Department
   2. Offered additional coding education
   3. Subject to focused audit if patterns persist
