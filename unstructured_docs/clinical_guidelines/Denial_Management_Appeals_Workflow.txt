SUBJECT: Standard Operating Procedures for Denial Prevention, Management, and Appeals

1. PURPOSE

This policy establishes the framework for managing claim denials across Grady Health System, including prevention strategies, workflow standards, escalation procedures, and performance metrics.

2. DENIAL CATEGORIZATION

All denials are categorized into the following groups for tracking and resolution:

2.1 Clinical Denials:
   - Medical necessity (CO-234, CO-50)
   - Diagnosis mismatch (CO-11)
   - Level of care disputes
   - Observation vs. inpatient status
   Responsible: Clinical Denials Team / Physician Advisors
   Target appeal rate: 85%
   Target overturn rate: 65%

2.2 Technical/Administrative Denials:
   - Missing information (CO-16)
   - Duplicate claims (CO-18)
   - Timely filing (CO-29)
   - Coordination of benefits (CO-22)
   Responsible: Billing Denials Team
   Target appeal rate: 70%
   Target overturn rate: 80%

2.3 Authorization Denials:
   - Prior authorization required (CO-197)
   - Authorization not obtained (CO-15)
   - Authorization expired
   Responsible: Authorization Team / Clinical Staff
   Target appeal rate: 90%
   Target overturn rate: 55%

2.4 Coding Denials:
   - Modifier issues (CO-4)
   - Bundling (CO-97)
   - Upcoding/downcoding
   Responsible: Coding Compliance Team
   Target appeal rate: 75%
   Target overturn rate: 70%

2.5 Contractual Denials:
   - Fee schedule (CO-45)
   - Patient responsibility (PR-1, PR-2, PR-3)
   Responsible: Contract Management Team
   Action: Verify against contract terms; adjust or appeal

3. DENIAL WORKFLOW

3.1 Identification (Day 0-1):
   - Electronic remittance advice (ERA) loaded into billing system daily
   - Denials automatically categorized by denial reason code
   - High-dollar denials (>$10,000) flagged for priority review
   - Dashboard updated with denial counts, amounts, and aging

3.2 Initial Review (Day 1-3):
   - Assigned analyst reviews denial within 3 business days
   - Determine if denial is valid (write off) or appealable
   - If appealable, gather required documentation
   - If correctable (e.g., CO-16), resubmit corrected claim immediately

3.3 Appeal Filing (Day 3-14):
   - First level appeals filed within 14 days of denial
   - All appeals must include:
     a) Cover letter citing specific denial reason and appeal basis
     b) Copy of denial notice / Explanation of Benefits
     c) Relevant medical records
     d) Supporting clinical documentation
     e) Peer-reviewed literature (for medical necessity appeals)
     f) Provider letter of medical necessity (when applicable)

3.4 Appeal Tracking (Day 14-60):
   - Follow up on pending appeals every 14 days
   - Document all follow-up activities (date, contact, reference number)
   - Escalate to second level if no response within 45 days

3.5 Resolution (Day 60-120):
   - Record appeal outcome (overturned, upheld, partial)
   - If overturned: verify payment posted correctly
   - If upheld: determine if second level appeal or external review is warranted
   - If write-off: document reason and obtain appropriate approval

4. PREVENTION STRATEGIES

4.1 Pre-Service:
   - Verify eligibility and benefits before every encounter
   - Obtain prior authorization for all required services
   - Document authorization numbers in the registration system
   - Perform medical necessity checks using clinical decision support tools

4.2 Point-of-Service:
   - Ensure complete and accurate documentation at the time of service
   - Use coding decision trees for E/M level selection
   - Complete physician attestations within 24 hours
   - Capture all charges within 3 days of service

4.3 Post-Service:
   - Submit clean claims within 48 hours of charge capture
   - Monitor claim status at 14-day intervals
   - Address rejected claims within 24 hours
   - Review underpayments against contracted rates

5. PERFORMANCE METRICS

5.1 Key Performance Indicators:
   - Overall denial rate: Target < 5% of net revenue
   - Initial denial rate: Target < 8% of claims submitted
   - Appeal rate: Target > 75% of eligible denials appealed
   - Appeal overturn rate: Target > 60%
   - Average days to appeal: Target < 14 days
   - Average days to resolution: Target < 45 days
   - Net write-off from denials: Target < 2% of net revenue

5.2 Reporting:
   - Daily: Denial volume and high-dollar alerts
   - Weekly: Denial trending by reason code, payer, department
   - Monthly: Comprehensive denial dashboard with financial impact
   - Quarterly: Root cause analysis and process improvement recommendations

6. ESCALATION MATRIX

   Denial Amount    | Approval Required     | Escalation Path
   < $5,000         | Denial Analyst        | Team Lead
   $5,000 - $25,000 | Team Lead             | Denial Manager
   $25,000 - $100K  | Denial Manager        | Director of Revenue Cycle
   > $100,000       | Director              | VP of Revenue Cycle / CFO

7. WRITE-OFF APPROVAL

   Amount           | Approval Authority
   < $1,000         | Denial Analyst
   $1,000 - $10,000 | Team Lead
   $10,000 - $50K   | Denial Manager
   > $50,000        | Director of Revenue Cycle
