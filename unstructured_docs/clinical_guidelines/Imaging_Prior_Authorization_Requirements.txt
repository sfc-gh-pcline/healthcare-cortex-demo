SUBJECT: Prior Authorization Requirements for Advanced Diagnostic Imaging

1. PURPOSE

This guideline outlines prior authorization requirements for advanced diagnostic imaging services to reduce CO-197 denials and ensure compliance with payer requirements. Advanced imaging includes MRI, CT, PET/CT, and nuclear medicine studies.

2. SERVICES REQUIRING PRIOR AUTHORIZATION

The following imaging services require prior authorization from MOST commercial payers and Medicaid managed care plans:

2.1 MRI Studies:
   - MRI Brain (CPT 70551, 70553)
   - MRI Spine - Cervical, Thoracic, Lumbar (CPT 72141-72158)
   - MRI Extremity (CPT 73218-73723)
   - MRI Abdomen/Pelvis (CPT 74181-74183)
   - MRI Cardiac (CPT 75557-75563)

2.2 CT Studies:
   - CT Head/Brain (CPT 70450-70460) - NOTE: NOT required for emergency presentations
   - CT Chest (CPT 71260-71270)
   - CT Abdomen/Pelvis (CPT 74176-74178)
   - CT Angiography (CPT 71275, 74174, 74175)

2.3 PET/CT Studies:
   - PET Brain (CPT 78816)
   - PET Whole Body (CPT 78815, 78816)
   - All PET studies require prior authorization regardless of payer

2.4 Nuclear Medicine:
   - Cardiac stress test - nuclear (CPT 78451, 78452)
   - Bone scan (CPT 78300, 78305)
   - Thyroid uptake and scan (CPT 78012, 78014)

3. EXEMPTIONS FROM PRIOR AUTHORIZATION

The following scenarios do NOT require prior authorization:
   - Emergency Department patients with acute presentations
   - Inpatient orders (covered under inpatient authorization)
   - X-rays (all standard radiographs)
   - Ultrasound studies
   - Mammography (screening and diagnostic)
   - Fluoroscopy
   - CT Head without contrast for emergency presentations (trauma, stroke, altered mental status)

5. CLINICAL DECISION SUPPORT REQUIREMENTS

5.1 CMS Appropriate Use Criteria (AUC):
   - As of January 2025, ordering providers must consult a qualified Clinical Decision Support Mechanism (CDSM) when ordering advanced imaging for Medicare patients
   - Grady uses [CDSM Vendor] integrated into the EHR ordering workflow
   - The CDSM consultation ID must be included on the claim
   - AUC adherence is a quality measure and does not affect payment (currently educational period)

5.2 Documentation Requirements for Authorization:
   - Clinical indication (signs, symptoms, relevant history)
   - Results of prior imaging (to demonstrate progression or need for advanced study)
   - Conservative treatment attempted (for musculoskeletal imaging)
   - Relevant lab results
   - Prior imaging results and dates

6. WORKFLOW FOR ORDERING PROVIDERS

Step 1: Order the study in the EHR
Step 2: CDSM automatically consulted (Medicare patients)
Step 3: Authorization team checks payer requirements
Step 4: If prior auth required:
   a) Authorization team submits request with clinical documentation
   b) If approved: study scheduled
   c) If denied: ordering provider notified for peer-to-peer review
   d) If peer-to-peer unsuccessful: appeal or alternative study considered
Step 5: If prior auth NOT required: study scheduled directly

7. DENIAL PREVENTION METRICS

Target denial rate for imaging CO-197: < 3%
Current baseline: 7.2% (as of Q4 2024)
Root causes identified:
   - 45% - Authorization obtained but not documented on claim
   - 30% - Authorization not obtained before study performed
   - 15% - Study performed differs from authorized study
   - 10% - Authorization expired before study date
